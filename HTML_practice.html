Tesla: Bringing the future

Tesla changed the way people thought about electric cars. Especially in 2012 when they released the Model S which proved that electric cars could have both useable range and look good. Through the years, they have innovated by developing their SUV, the Model X, and stepped down in price range in both the sedan and suv markets culminating in the Model 3 sedan and Model Y suv. By stepping down in price, they opened up the market and forced the hands of other manufacturers into trying to grab a piece of the electric market.

Tesla: Unmatched Performance

For the 2021 model year, Tesla once again threw down the performance gauntlet to all other manufacturers by releasing the Model S Plaid. With a 0-60 time of 1.9 seconds and a quarter mile time of 9.2 seconds, it leaves behind all internal combustion cars offered by the manufacturers at EVERY price point. When you consider the starting price of $129,000, which of course no small amount, the Model S Plaid could arguably be the performance car bargain of this decade.
